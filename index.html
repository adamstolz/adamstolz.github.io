Who We Are

Stolz Management Advisors LLC provides a range of advisory services to mission-driven organizations.
We help those who lead these organizations to anticipate, understand, and address their toughest long-term challenges.

Adam Stolz

Founding Principal

Adam Stolz has over ten years of experience as a manager and strategist for mission-driven organizations. He has worked across a range of disciplines, with a focus on the healthcare and public safety sectors. Mr. Stolz’s experience includes devising enterprise strategy, designing and evaluating programs, conducting financial analyses, improving governance and performance, coordinating complex field operations, and forming institutional partnerships.

Mr. Stolz is the founding principal of Stolz Management Advisors. Previously, he served as an Engagement Manager with The Chartis Group, a national healthcare management consultancy. In both his current and former roles, Mr. Stolz has focused on helping organizations that provide healthcare services develop enterprise strategy, redesign care delivery networks and organizational structures, form affiliations and ventures, and attain long-term financial sustainability. He has developed strategy for national not-for-profit and for-profit health systems, academic medical centers, children’s hospitals, and community health centers. He has also worked to implement health policy reforms at the state level.

Prior to his work in the healthcare sector, Mr. Stolz held several positions, including First Deputy Director, for the Baltimore City Mayor’s Office of Emergency Management. In that role he oversaw daily operations and managed citywide planning, training, and exercise programs. He chaired Baltimore’s Local Emergency Planning Committee and worked with business leaders, non-profit organizations, and other jurisdictions to build capacity and align procedures for responding to disasters. Mr. Stolz helped manage Baltimore’s response to over 30 major incidents including hurricanes, hazardous materials releases, water and power outages, and H1N1 influenza. He was appointed by the Governor of Maryland to serve on the Governor’s Emergency Management Advisory Council. Among his public safety certifications, Mr. Stolz is an Emergency Medical Technician, Wilderness First Responder, and Incident Command System Instructor.

Mr. Stolz earned a Master of Public Administration degree from the University of Michigan’s Gerald R. Ford School of Public Policy. He holds a Bachelor of Arts degree with honors from the Johns Hopkins University.
